# Underwriting Memo for BNPL Merchant Portfolio

## Executive Summary
The BNPL merchant portfolio exhibits a moderate risk profile, with an expected high-risk count of 3.42 and an average predicted risk of 0.0684. The expected loss proxy is estimated at $5,181.20. Given these findings, the recommendation is to **Approve with Conditions**.

## Data Sources & Methodology
Data was sourced from a combination of CSV files, mock APIs, REST Countries, and a ClarityPay scrape. The risk model was constructed using out-of-fold evaluation with two baseline models, Logistic Regression and Random Forest.

## Model Comparison
The Random Forest model was chosen over Logistic Regression due to its superior ROC AUC score of 0.5 compared to 0.4667. Both models exhibited low precision, recall, and F1 scores, indicating challenges in risk classification.

## Calibration & Probability Quality
Calibration metrics show a Brier score of 0.081316. Predicted probabilities are roughly aligned with observed outcomes, but the small sample size limits confidence. Calibration is crucial for setting underwriting thresholds, as a 0.3 probability should correspond to a ~30% observed high-risk rate.

## Recommendations & Controls
- Implement manual reviews for merchants with a probability of high risk greater than 0.5.
- Conduct manual reviews for merchants flagged as high risk internally.
- Monitor dispute rates closely and adjust risk thresholds as necessary.
- Regularly update the risk model with new data to improve accuracy.

### Underwriting Conditions
- Manual review for merchants with prob_high_risk > 0.5.
- Manual review for merchants with internal_risk_flag == high.

## Caveats & Assumptions
- Sample data is not representative of production.
- High dispute risk is defined as a dispute rate > 0.002.
- Expected loss proxy uses a 2% assumed loss rate on at-risk volume.
- Calibration results are illustrative only due to a very small dataset.

**Recommendation: Approve with Conditions**
